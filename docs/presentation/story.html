Chapter 02 · Business storytelling

From the lab notebook to the operations desk

Persona: a sports analyst or desk that needs repeatable pre-match win probabilities — not a one-off chart from last Tuesday’s Jupyter session. Conflict: notebooks explore; they don’t govern. Resolution: our MLOps stack turns the same science into a product: API, UI, tests, registry, logs.

The drama: two worlds

Notebook-heavy PoC

Hidden cell order. “Works on my machine.” Which pickle file is production? No contract on JSON inputs. Stakeholders can’t self-serve; every question routes through a data scientist. Business cost: latency, key-person risk, un-auditable decisions.

Our MLOps product

Single command pipeline. Pydantic validates every API call. Model promoted via W&B prod. Streamlit + Swagger let anyone reproduce the same prediction. Business value: trust, speed, and a paper trail when regulators or risk ask “why this score?”

Narrative arc (for your 10′ pitch)

  • Act I — Pain. Gut feel and ad-hoc notebooks don’t scale; calibration matters more than a flashy accuracy slide.
  • Act II — Pivot. We engineered the same ATP prediction into modules, tests, and a deployed API — so “model” means one governed artifact.
  • Act III — Proof. Live /docs, Streamlit, W&B lineage, CI green — evidence, not slides.
  • Denouement — Honesty. We state limitations (no in-match stats, drift) and position the system as decision support, especially in betting-adjacent contexts.

Why MLOps = higher business value (summary)

Dimension Notebook workflow MLOps workflow
